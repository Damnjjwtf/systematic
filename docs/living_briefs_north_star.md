# Living Briefs: North Star

## Product Thesis
Living Briefs are source-grounded intelligence products you can talk to.

A normal brief is consumed. A Living Brief is queried, challenged, updated, remembered, and turned into action.

Systematic is the first expression of this category:

```text
Systematic - The Living Brief for Systematic Traders
```

## Why This Matters
Most daily briefs summarize what happened.

Living Briefs should help users reason with what happened.

They should answer:

- What changed?
- Why does it matter?
- What should I ignore?
- What is weakly sourced?
- Have we seen this before?
- What changed in the thesis?
- What should I do, avoid, or monitor next?

The product is not "AI chat over content." The product is a disciplined, source-grounded reasoning object with memory.

## Core Interaction
The core interaction is:

```text
Ask the Brief
```

Not "Ask AI." Not "Chat." The user is talking to the brief itself.

The brief should be able to answer questions like:

- Is this relevant if I only trade trend?
- Which source is weakest?
- What part of this should I ignore?
- Compare this to yesterday.
- What has changed over the last 30 days?
- What would the Risk lens say?
- Turn this into a checklist.
- Show me only the regulatory items.
- What should I not do?

## Product Levels
## Level 1: Brief As Email
Fast, scannable, source-grounded, and useful in under 3 minutes.

This is the v1 surface.

## Level 2: Brief As Object
Each brief is structured data:

- findings
- sources
- source tiers
- caveats
- confidence
- future vectors
- watchlist items
- recommended moves
- persona takes
- archive memory

This makes the brief inspectable instead of disposable.

## Level 3: Brief As Conversation
The user can ask questions constrained to:

- today's brief
- source metadata
- archive memory
- the brief's stated thesis
- the user's chosen lens

The constraint is the moat. It keeps Living Briefs from becoming generic chatbots.

## Design Principles
- Source-grounded before fluent.
- Memory before novelty.
- Caveats visible by default.
- Ask the Brief, not Ask AI.
- Useful restraint beats infinite interactivity.
- The brief may suggest how to improve itself.
- The meta layer should serve the reader, not become inside-baseball.
- No personalized financial advice.
- No pretending uncertainty is certainty.

## Why Systematic Is A Good First Living Brief
Systematic traders do not just need news. They need:

- discipline
- regime context
- source quality
- drawdown memory
- implementation checks
- operational risk alerts
- research triage
- protection from reactive decision-making

That makes Systematic a strong wedge. The user is not asking for more content. They are asking for a calmer, smarter reasoning layer.

## Long-Term Moat
The archive compounds.

Each issue adds:

- source reputation
- repeated signals
- stale narratives
- useful questions
- prior caveats
- reader feedback
- recurring watches

Over time, the brief becomes less like a newsletter and more like a domain-specific intelligence partner.
